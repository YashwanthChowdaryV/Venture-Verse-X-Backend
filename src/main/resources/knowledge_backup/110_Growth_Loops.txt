CORE CONCEPT

Growth Loops are self-reinforcing systems where the output of one cycle of the growth process becomes the input for the next cycle, creating compounding growth without requiring proportional increases in input. The core concept, developed by the Reforge program and Brian Balfour, is that loops are fundamentally different from funnels. A funnel is linear: input goes in, some percentage comes out, and to get more output you need more input. A loop is cyclical: the output of the process feeds back into the input, so growth compounds without requiring linear increases in input. The failure mode is "Funnel Thinking": optimizing a linear acquisition funnel without identifying and building the loops that can create compounding growth. For investors, a startup's growth loops are a signal of whether growth can compound sustainably. A company with strong growth loops has a compounding engine; a company dependent on linear funnels will eventually hit diminishing returns.

FRAMEWORK & MECHANICS

The Growth Loops framework identifies three primary loop types: User-Generated Content Loops (users create content that attracts new users who create more content -- YouTube, TikTok, Pinterest), Viral/Referral Loops (users invite new users who invite more users -- Dropbox, WhatsApp, Slack), and Data/Product Loops (usage generates data that improves the product, which attracts more users, which generates more data -- Google Search, Spotify, Tesla). Each loop has three components: Input (what starts the loop -- a new user, a piece of content, a data point), Action/Process (what the input does -- creates content, invites friends, generates data), and Output (what the loop produces -- more users, more content, more data, more revenue).

The operational mechanism is the "Loop Mapping": the growth team maps the company's growth loops, identifies the key metrics that drive each loop (loop input, loop action, loop output), measures the loop's performance, and optimizes the bottleneck in the loop. The mental model is "growth is a system of loops, not a set of channels." Channels (paid acquisition, content marketing, sales) are inputs to loops; loops are the systems that compound those inputs. The decision process: the growth team identifies the company's primary growth loop, measures its health, and invests in strengthening the loop before adding new loops.

GROWTH EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

Healthy growth loop execution: the company has identified and mapped its primary growth loop; the loop metrics are measured and understood; the loop is compounding (each cycle generates more output than input); the company is investing in strengthening the loop rather than just adding more inputs. Unhealthy: the company has not mapped its growth loops; growth is dependent on linear inputs (more ad spend, more salespeople); the loop is not compounding. Growth quality indicators: the loop's compounding rate; the percentage of growth driven by loops vs. linear inputs; the efficiency of the loop (output per unit of input). Growth sustainability indicators: the number of loops; the health of each loop; the diversity of loops.

KEY METRICS & BENCHMARKS

Benchmark 1: Loop Compounding Rate. The percentage by which each loop cycle increases the input for the next cycle. Above 10% is strong compounding. Warning: below 0% (the loop is declining). Benchmark 2: Loop-Driven Growth Percentage. The percentage of total growth driven by loops vs. linear inputs. Above 50% is strong. Warning: below 20%. Benchmark 3: Loop Cycle Time. The time for one complete cycle of the loop. Under 1 month is strong. Warning: above 3 months. Benchmark 4: Loop Efficiency. The output of the loop divided by the external input. Above 2x is strong. Warning: below 1x. Benchmark 5: Number of Active Loops. At least one primary loop at scale. Warning: zero mapped loops. Benchmark 6: Loop Bottleneck Metric. The specific metric that is constraining the loop's growth. Should be identified and improving.

REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE

Pinterest's growth loops are a masterclass in user-generated content loops. Pinterest's primary loop: users pin images (content creation), which appear in search results and feeds (content distribution), which attract new users (acquisition), who pin more images (content creation). The loop is powered by Pinterest's investment in visual search technology, which improves as more images are pinned. The outcome: Pinterest grew to over 450 million monthly active users with a growth loop that compounds as more users create more content. The investor lesson: Pinterest's growth is not dependent on increasing ad spend; it is powered by a loop where each new user makes the platform more valuable for the next user.

COMMON MISTAKES

The first mistake is "Funnel Optimization Without Loop Building": optimizing the conversion funnel without identifying the loops that can make growth compound. The second mistake is "Single-Loop Dependency": relying on a single growth loop that could be disrupted. The third mistake is "No Loop Measurement": claiming loops without measuring the loop metrics (compounding rate, cycle time, efficiency). The fourth mistake is "Input-Only Growth": treating growth as entirely dependent on inputs (more ad spend, more salespeople) without building loops that compound those inputs. The fifth mistake is "Loop Confusion with Channel": confusing a loop (a self-reinforcing system) with a channel (a source of users).

COUNTERINTUITIVE INSIGHT

The most powerful growth loops are often the ones that are invisible to the growth team because they are embedded in the product. A product feature that causes users to create content, which attracts more users, who create more content, is a growth loop even if the growth team did not design it as one. The job of the growth team is to identify, measure, and strengthen these loops.

INVESTOR PERSPECTIVE

Positive loop signals: identified and mapped growth loops; measured loop metrics; compounding growth; a majority of growth from loops; multiple loops at scale. Red flags: no mapped loops; growth entirely dependent on linear inputs; loops claimed but not measured; single loop without diversification. Due diligence questions: "What are your growth loops, and how do they work?" "What percentage of your growth is driven by loops vs. linear inputs?" "How do you measure the health of your growth loops?" "What is the bottleneck in your primary loop, and how are you addressing it?" "What new loops are you building?"

GROWTH DIAGNOSTICS

Positive Signals: Mapped loops; measured metrics; compounding growth; loop-driven above 50%; multiple loops. Warning Signals: No mapped loops; linear-only growth; loops not measured; loop-driven below 20%. Critical Thresholds: No mapped loops; loop-driven below 10%; loop declining. Leading Indicators: loop input growth, loop action rate, loop cycle time. Lagging Indicators: loop-driven growth percentage, loop compounding rate, overall growth rate. Root Causes of loop failure: not identified, not measured, bottleneck not addressed, over-reliance on inputs. Corrective Actions: map the loops; measure the metrics; identify and fix the bottleneck; build new loops.

ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS

1. Map your company's growth loops. Identify the self-reinforcing systems where output becomes input. If you cannot identify any, you are dependent on linear inputs.
2. Measure each loop's compounding rate, cycle time, and efficiency. If you cannot measure them, you cannot improve them.
3. Identify the bottleneck in your primary loop. What single constraint is limiting the loop's growth? Focus investment on removing that constraint.
4. Build a dashboard that tracks loop metrics alongside funnel metrics. The loop metrics are the leading indicators of sustainable growth.
5. Diversify your loops. Aim for at least two growth loops at scale, so that a disruption to one does not stop growth.
6. Design new product features with loop potential: how can this feature create output that becomes input for the next cycle?
7. Shift investment from linear inputs (more ad spend) to loop strengthening (improving the loop's efficiency) as the company matures.
8. For investors: Request the growth loop map and the loop metrics. A founder who cannot articulate their growth loops is operating on linear inputs.
