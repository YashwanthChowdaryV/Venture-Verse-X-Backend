CORE CONCEPT

The VentureVerse Risk Assessment Framework (RAF) is the systematic methodology for identifying, quantifying, aggregating, and monitoring the risks that could impair or destroy a startup's value. The core concept is that risk assessment is not a checklist; it is a structured, scored, continuously updated evaluation that distinguishes between acceptable risks (inherent to the opportunity) and unacceptable risks (avoidable or unpriceable). The RAF produces a Risk Score that feeds into the VentureVerse Investment Score and enables the Risk Agent to generate risk alerts, monitor risk trajectories, and recommend mitigations. The failure mode for risk assessment is "Risk as a Narrative": treating risk as a qualitative discussion rather than a quantified, scored, and tracked dimension. VentureVerse agents use the RAF to make risk explicit, comparable, and actionable.

VENTUREVERSE FRAMEWORK

The RAF operates through a three-dimensional risk evaluation model. Risk Identification: cataloging all material risks across eight categories -- Founder Risk, Team Risk, Product Risk, Market Risk, Competitive Risk, Financial Risk, Technology Risk, and Regulatory Risk. Risk Quantification: scoring each identified risk on three dimensions -- Probability (1-10 scale, how likely is the risk to materialize?), Severity (1-10 scale, if it materializes, how damaging would it be?), and Velocity (how quickly could it materialize -- immediate, months, years?). Risk Aggregation: combining individual risk scores into Category Risk Scores and an overall RAF Score, accounting for risk interaction (the degree to which risks could compound). The framework applies a "detection premium": risks that are detected early and have credible mitigants receive a lower effective score than risks that are detected late or have no mitigants. The framework also applies a "controllability adjustment": risks that are controllable by the company are discounted; risks that are external and uncontrollable are amplified.

SCORING METHODOLOGY

Each identified risk is scored on Probability (1-10), Severity (1-10), and Velocity (Immediate=3, Months=2, Years=1). The Individual Risk Score = Probability x Severity x Velocity Multiplier. Scores above 200 are critical; 100-200 are elevated; 50-100 are moderate; below 50 are low. The Category Risk Score is the weighted average of Individual Risk Scores in that category, with an interaction multiplier applied if risks within the category could compound. The overall RAF Score is the weighted average of Category Risk Scores, with weights reflecting the stage-specific importance of each risk category. The RAF Score is normalized to 0-100 (inverted: higher score means lower risk). The formula: RAF Score = 100 - (Weighted Risk Score / Maximum Possible Weighted Risk Score x 100). A mitigant discount of up to 30% is applied to individual risks with credible mitigants. A controllability adjustment of up to 20% is applied based on the proportion of risks that are controllable.

WEIGHTING MODEL

The RAF weighting model is stage-specific. At Seed stage: Product Risk (25%), Founder Risk (20%), Market Risk (20%), Financial Risk (15%), Team Risk (10%), Competitive Risk (5%), Technology Risk (3%), Regulatory Risk (2%). At Series A: Product Risk (20%), Market Risk (20%), Financial Risk (20%), Team Risk (15%), Competitive Risk (10%), Founder Risk (5%), Technology Risk (5%), Regulatory Risk (5%). At Growth stage: Competitive Risk (25%), Financial Risk (20%), Market Risk (15%), Team Risk (15%), Regulatory Risk (10%), Product Risk (5%), Technology Risk (5%), Founder Risk (5%). Within each category, individual risk weights are based on the Individual Risk Score, with higher-scoring risks receiving proportionally more weight.

INPUT SIGNALS

Positive signals: risks well-understood, credible mitigants in place, high proportion of controllable risks, low risk interaction, risks declining over time, founder proactively surfacing risks. Negative signals: risks poorly understood, no mitigants, high proportion of uncontrollable risks, high risk interaction, risks increasing over time, founder hiding or dismissing risks. Missing signals are treated as elevated risk (assume the worst until evidence is available). Contradictory signals (e.g., founder claims low risk but evidence suggests high risk) amplify the risk score.

DECISION LOGIC

RAF Score above 80: Low risk (investable, standard terms). RAF Score 65-80: Moderate risk (investable with monitoring and specific mitigants). RAF Score 50-65: Elevated risk (investable only with structural protections: lower valuation, tranched investment, specific milestones). RAF Score 35-50: High risk (significant concerns; investment only in exceptional circumstances). RAF Score below 35: Critical risk (likely uninvestable). Any Individual Risk Score above 200 triggers a mandatory review, regardless of the overall RAF Score. Any Integrity risk signal triggers an automatic Critical designation. The RAF Score feeds into the VIS as the Risk Category Score (inverted: a high RAF Score contributes positively to the VIS).

REAL-WORLD APPLICATION

Consider a fintech startup: Regulatory Risk (Probability 7, Severity 9, Velocity Months = Score 189, elevated), Market Risk (Probability 4, Severity 6, Velocity Years = Score 24, low), Financial Risk (Probability 3, Severity 7, Velocity Years = Score 21, low), Product Risk (Probability 3, Severity 5, Velocity Years = Score 15, low). The Regulatory Risk is elevated and drives the risk profile. However, the company has engaged regulatory counsel, obtained preliminary approvals, and has a credible compliance plan, earning a mitigant discount of 25%, reducing the effective Regulatory Risk Score to 142. The Category Risk Scores are weighted by stage (Series A), producing a weighted Risk Score of 85. The RAF Score is 100 - (85/300 x 100) = 72, placing it in the Moderate risk range. The recommendation is investable with monitoring on regulatory developments and a requirement for regulatory milestones.

COMMON SCORING ERRORS

The first error is "Risk Optimism": underestimating probability and severity due to excitement about the opportunity. The second is "Single-Risk Focus": focusing on one risk category while ignoring others. The third is "Ignoring Interaction": evaluating risks in isolation without considering compounding. The fourth is "Static Assessment": treating risk as fixed rather than dynamic. The fifth is "Mitigant Overestimation": accepting the company's mitigant claims without independent validation.

COUNTERINTUITIVE INSIGHT

The most dangerous risk profile is not the one with the highest individual risk but the one with multiple moderate risks that can interact. A startup with three moderate risks (scores of 80 each) that are interconnected is more dangerous than a startup with one high risk (score of 200) that is isolated. The RAF's interaction multiplier captures this dynamic.

INVESTMENT INTERPRETATION

The RAF Score is a risk assessment, not a return assessment. A high-risk startup may still be an attractive investment if the expected return compensates for the risk. The RAF should be interpreted alongside the VIS and the expected return analysis. A startup with a low RAF Score but a very high VIS in other categories may represent an asymmetric opportunity. The RAF is most reliable when risks are well-understood and quantifiable. It is less reliable for novel technologies or markets where risks are unknown.

SYSTEM DIAGNOSTICS

Positive Signals: Risks well-understood, credible mitigants, high controllability, low interaction, declining risk trajectory, founder transparency. Warning Signals: Risks poorly understood, no mitigants, low controllability, high interaction, increasing risk trajectory, founder evasiveness. Confidence Indicators: risk identification completeness, mitigant validation, data source quality. Data Quality Indicators: independent vs. company-reported risk assessment. Model Risk Indicators: unknown risks, risk interaction uncertainty. Recommendation Reliability Indicators: historical correlation between RAF Scores and adverse outcomes.

VENTUREVERSE AGENT REASONING

The Risk Agent identifies, quantifies, and monitors risks, generating the RAF Score and risk alerts. It monitors risk trajectories and triggers alerts when risks increase above thresholds. The Investor Agent incorporates the RAF Score as the Risk Category Score in the VIS. The Due Diligence Agent uses the RAF to prioritize diligence areas: high-risk categories receive deeper diligence. When the Risk Agent and Investor Agent disagree (e.g., high risk but strong other scores), the conflict is flagged for human review. The Risk Agent provides a narrative explanation of the RAF, including the top risks, their interactions, the mitigants, and the trajectory.

7. Integrate the RAF Score into the VIS as the Risk Category Score (inverted).
